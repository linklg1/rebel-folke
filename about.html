Meet the Artists (and their work)



Shae Segagkind




Shae Seqaqkind (She/Her) is an Irish and Lenape woman living on unceded & stolen xʷməθkʷəy̓əm, Sḵwx̱wú7mesh, and sƏl̓ílwƏtaʔɬ lands. She is a practicing artist, art therapist, registered psychotherapist (qualifying), and art educator. Shae is passionate about fostering creativity in people of all ages and thinks that creativity is the closest thing that we have to magic. Shae hopes that the work she creates brings joy to people’s spaces. Follow Shae on Instagram




Ta




Ta (they/she) currently lives, plays, and works on the stolen lands of the xʷməθkʷəy̓əm, Sḵwx̱wú7mesh, səlilwətaɬ, and Stó:lō peoples. They are a Thai citizen with Tai, Southern Chinese, and Mon ancestry. Beyond their current focus on decolonial environmental justice and animal rights advocacy, they hope for Land Back and the end to all systems of oppression. Ta learned how to bead from an Indigenous youth. They want to share these original beaded earring designs with those who stand with Palestine. Follow Ta on Instagram




Jasper




Jasper grew up in Saskatchewan but has been living in Vancouver for a bit now. Jasper’s painting is acrylic paint with permanent markers. Jasper was inspired by a street they walk by often where a Palestinian flag is proudly waving right next to the busy traffic for everyone to see.




Link




Link is a queer-trans Palestinian-Jordanian settler in “Vancouver”. They have taken time away from poetry but this piece has been a long standing favourite they’ve written. The inspiration for the poem came from dreams and childhood memories as well as Links life long struggle with depression and anxiety. Link is involved in the Free Palestine movement through the obligation that calls us all to work to create a just world. You can see them biking around the city with flags raised, keffiyeh on and music blasting. They hope this poem will bring comfort to those who share similar struggles. Follow Link on Instagram




Mia




Mia has been crafting all their life. They reside on xʷməθkʷəy̓əm, Sḵwx̱wú7mesh, and sƏl̓ílwƏtaʔɬ land with their wife and two cats. Mia believes that the core Jewish principle of Tikkun Olam—healing the world—must include Palestinian liberation, and she is honoured to be participating in this project. Follow Mia on Instagram




LC




LC is a Trans interdisciplinary settler artist working in unsurrendered xwmǝθkwǝyǝ̓ m, Sḵwx̱wú7mesh, and sƏl ̓ ílwƏtaʔɬ & Lenape Lands. Their practice revolves around pattern (language), tentacularity, waste/value and Containership. Follow LC on Instagram Each piece is made from 100% reclaimed & salvaged material including; glass, ceramic, iron, ink, pencil, and cardboard. The 3D pieces can be used as a dishes, jewelry holders, garden decor, or whatever you want! They all feature organic colour variation and pattern, text (including the arabic word بيت )




Greta Burley




Greta is a queer artist and maker residing on the traditional, ancestral and unceded territory of the xʷməθkʷəy̓əm, Sḵwx̱wú7mesh, and səlilwətaɬ Nations. She is passionate about collective liberation and social justice issues and building community through skill sharing, art and teaching. Follow Greta on Instagram




Sara




Sara was born and raised in Mexico City. She has made a portrait of Leila Khaled, as the first secretary of the Palestine women’s organization her imagconveys liberation for Palestine and the immense strength and power of womanhood. Follow Sara on Instagram




Allison Chow




Allison Chow is an artist and writer. Her practice draws from her studies of visual language, communications and a lifelong passion for disappearing into literary worlds. She lives on the unsurrendered lands of the Musqueam, Squamish and Tsleil-Waututh Nations. Find her at hellochowder.com and on Instagram
